Network

By Jonathan Rogers and Matthew Gatland

This is an unfinished version of a 2 player strategy game. There's no tutorial yet, so here are some instructions:

Story

Two utility companies compete to supply roads, water, power and internet to the most customers.

Controls

  • Command-R to restart.
  • Click on a highlighted edge, then choose an option from the pop-up menu.
  • You can keep building until you run out of cards or no moves are possible.
  • Click on 'end turn' to end your turn and let the other player make their move.

Building

At the bottom of the screen are your build cards. You can use these to build roads, water pipes, electricity lines and internet fibre.

Wildcards can be used to build anything.

You start with three road cards. Every turn, you receive one wildcard.

In your turn, click on any highlighted edge to build a new connection. You can build connections until you run out of cards. Then click 'End Turn' to let the other player take their turn.

You must start the game by building a road from a road source on the edge of the board.

You can activate a water source, electricity source or internet source by building a road to it.

You can build pipes, lines and fibre to expand the reach of your sources.

Only one type of connection can be built on each edge. For example, you cannot build a power line on top of a road. However, two different edge types can cross over each other at a corner.

Cities

Cities will grow in squares that are supplied with services:

  • Road - no city
  • Road and water - Level 1 City
  • Road, water and electricity - Level 2 City
  • Road, water, electricity and internet - Level 3 City

If you increase the level of at least one city in your turn, you will receive a bonus card to use next turn. Therefore you should try to increase the level of at least one city in every turn.

Note that higher level services only work once lower level services are provided. For example, internet gives no benefit until electricity is provided.

Cities will grow even if different players provide the required services. For example, one player can provide the road while another provides water.

Disconnecting and reconnecting

If you have a road that touches part of your opponent's network, you can destroy your opponent's edges. This costs one card of the same type (i.e. to destroy a fibre edge, you need one fibre card or one wildcard.)

If, by destroying an edge, you disconnect part of your opponent's network, that part of the network will become unowned. If your network connects to an unowned network, it will become yours.

Generator Cities

A level 3 city on a green square becomes a generator city. Every turn, it provides one additional wildcard to the player who supplies internet to that city. It does not matter who provides the other services to that city.

Providing internet to generator cities is the key to winning the game.

Ending the game

The game ends when one player cannot make any moves.

When the game ends, the player with the highest score wins. Scoring is based on providing services to cities:

  • Road - 0 points
  • Water - 1 point
  • Electricity - 2 points
  • Internet - 4 points

Not that services only work if all lower-level services are also provided. For example, providing internet to a city only gives points if a road, water and electricity are also provided.

If you have a higher score than your opponent, you can try to end the game by blocking your opponent in so they cannot make any more moves.
